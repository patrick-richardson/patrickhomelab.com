Troubleshooting Storage Capacity

Storage Infrastructure Troubleshooting

Primary NVMe Datastore Capacity Exhaustion Risk Overview The Forge Proxmox hypervisor began approaching the NVMe datastore capacity limits due to increased VM disk utilization and the expansion of infrastructure services. To restore available capacity and improve long-term storage management, selected virtual machine workloads were migrated from the primary NVMe datastore to a dedicated SSD-based datastore. Environment Environment: - Hypervisor: Forge (Proxmox VE) - Primary Storage: NVMe datastore - Secondary Storage: Dedicated SSD datastore - Affected Services: - media-01 - monitoring infrastructure - supporting lab VMs Problem The primary NVMe datastore was nearing operational capacity thresholds, creating several infrastructure risks: - Reduced capacity for future VM deployments - Increased risk of datastore exhaustion - Limited flexibility for snapshots and maintenance operations - Reduced storage headroom for infrastructure growth Continued growth in utilization without reallocation would have negatively impacted long-term hypervisor stability and storage management flexibility. Objective The objective of the migration was to redistribute VM storage workloads to a dedicated SSD datastore in order to: Restore available NVMe capacity - Improve storage allocation strategy - Preserve operational stability - Maintain service availability during migration - Improve future scalability of the lab environment Migration Actions Actions Performed: - Provisioned SSD datastore within Proxmox - Evaluated VM storage utilization - Selected workloads appropriate for migration - Migrated VM disks from NVMe storage to SSD storage - Validated successful VM boot operations - Verified service availability post-migration - Confirmed restored datastore capacity levels Validation Post-Migration Validation: - VM startup behavior confirmed - Plex services operational - Storage mounts verified - No data loss observed - Hypervisor storage utilization was reduced successfully - Infrastructure services remained stable after migration Result The migration successfully reduced pressure on the primary NVMe datastore while maintaining operational continuity across infrastructure services. The updated storage allocation strategy improved capacity management and restored additional headroom for future infrastructure expansion. Lessons Learned https://kb.patrickhomelab.com/link/4#bkmrk-lessons-learned%3A--ea Lessons Learned: - Early storage planning is critical for virtualization environments - Infrastructure growth can rapidly impact datastore utilization - Segregating workloads across multiple storage tiers improves flexibility - Capacity monitoring should be incorporated into routine infrastructure management workflows
